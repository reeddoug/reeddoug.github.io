I am a graduate student in Finance and Business Economics at the USC Marshall School of Business. I am interested in empirical asset pricing, dividend processes, and market micro-structure, all with the goal of understanding why investors price firms differently. I am currently working on a project examining the demand for dividends by type of investor, as well as two projects studying the cross-section of price-dividend ratios in place of returns. In a recent working paper, I find that price-ratios of individual firms do not appear well defined by return and dividend expectations, and instead are better explained by financial market frictions and an often ignored constant resulting from the Taylor approximation.
